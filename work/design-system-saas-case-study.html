Background

Capillary Technologies specializes in end-to-end loyalty programs with unified, cross-channel strategies for personalized, real-time customer experiences. The company serves over 875 million consumers and processes 1.97 billion+ transactions.

Key products include:

  • Loyalty+: Enhances customer relationships through personalized loyalty programs.
  • Engage+: AI-powered platform for consumer engagement.
  • Insights+: Advanced decision-making tool for better business insights.
  • Capillary CDP+: Consumer data platform for leveraging data to improve engagement.

In a system this large, a well-maintained design system is crucial. It ensures consistency across products, speeds up development, and creates a seamless experience for both users and teams. A strong design system becomes the backbone of efficiency and cohesion across every touchpoint.

Scope and Considerations

As a solo designer, my time was limited for developing a comprehensive Design System while juggling rapid UX projects. I quickly realized the Design System needed to scale efficiently to accommodate new use cases. To tackle this challenge, I adopted a strategic approach.

Collaboration was key. I engaged with other designers and developers to uncover their pain points, ensuring their needs were front and center. Together, we established both short-term and long-term priorities, with a focus on rapid scalability and modularity as guiding principles.

Design Audit & Repository

I began by conducting a design audit of the product to gain a clear understanding of the existing landscape. This involved creating a comprehensive repository that included:

  • Important screens
  • Key components and their variants
  • Usage patterns of these components across different screens

This audit allowed me to identify inconsistencies, spot gaps in the design system and set a baseline for improvement. It provided the foundation needed to streamline the system and ensure future scalability.

Laying the Foundations

One of my initial tasks was to establish a cohesive typography and color palette. Although the typography was already defined, it had numerous variants that often confused designers. To simplify this, I focused on:

  • Streamlining typography: Reduced the number of variants, making it easier for the team to remember when to use each one.

The existing color palette also had issues, as it failed to meet contrast ratio standards. I created a new palette that built upon the existing colors, ensuring both accessibility and aesthetic appeal.

Next, I developed foundational page templates. Collaborating closely with front-end engineers, we established a grid system for key breakpoints that aligned with their setup.

Building the Component Library

The next step was to develop a dynamic set of components, following the Atomic Design approach. I began by defining the global atoms and base components like Profile, Pill, Tag, Icon, Checkbox, Radio buttons, etc. This serves as the building blocks for larger components.

These foundational components were selected because they are frequently used across other primary components. This approach ensured consistency and scalability as the design system continued to grow.
